## What it is

Become a renowned Egyptologist in the 1920s. Navigate the desert, excavate ruins, trade with locals, and race rival archaeologists to legendary artifacts. An icon-driven, 12-bit aesthetic game aiming for the sweet spot between Commodore 64 and Amiga.

## What's in it

- **Fog-of-war exploration** across a hand-built map of Egypt
- **Resource management** — food, water, money, supplies
- **Random encounters** — locals, rivals, weather, bandits
- **Legendary artifacts** hidden in ruins, each with its own clue chain
- **Risk vs. reward** — every expedition is a budget decision with consequences

## Why

Heart of Africa and Seven Cities of Gold hit a specific nerve in 1980s-kid me that nothing has quite replicated since. This is my attempt at that feeling, in a setting I find endlessly fun.
