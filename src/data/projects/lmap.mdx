## What it is

You run a struggling Hollywood studio at the tail end of the silent era. Greenlight scripts, cast talent, manage budgets, court the press, keep the censors happy, and try to win an Oscar — all while a slow-burn murder mystery unspools around you.

It's a management game first and a mystery second. The mystery is woven through the day-to-day so that playing the studio well is also how you uncover what happened.

## Systems

- **Production pipeline** — scripts → greenlight → casting → shooting → post → release → box office → prestige.
- **Talent** — directors, actors, writers. Moods, relationships, and contracts. Hire, fire, fix.
- **Events** — random on-set incidents with cascading consequences you can handle through your press office, your fixer, or nobody at all.
- **The Academy** — year-end awards ceremony tracks nominations and wins across your whole roster.
- **The PCA** — 1934's Production Code Administration can send scripts back for rewrites. Pick your battles.
- **The mystery** — clues surface through events, dialogue, and the environment. You don't *have* to solve it to play, but you'll probably want to.

## Why I'm making it

I love management games and I love mystery games, and I kept thinking about what it would look like if one hid inside the other. The retro look isn't nostalgia bait — it's the most honest way to render the feel of a black-and-white-era studio on a budget of one.
